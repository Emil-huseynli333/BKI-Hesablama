1. What is Body Mass Index (BMI)?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple index of weight-for-height that is commonly used to classify underweight, overweight and obesity in adults. It is defined as a person's weight in kilograms divided by the square of his height in meters (kg/m²).

Formula: BMI = Weight (kg) / (Height (m) * Height (m))

3. Limitations of BMI: What It Doesn't Measure

While BMI is a useful screening tool, it does not diagnose body fatness or health. For example, athletes with high muscle mass may have a high BMI without having excess body fat. It also varies by age and pregnancy status. Therefore, it should be used as a general guide rather than a clinical diagnosis.

4. How to Reach a Healthy Weight?

  • Balanced Diet: Calorie intake should match your daily energy expenditure.
  • Physical Activity: At least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week is recommended.
  • Hydration: Drinking enough water is essential for a healthy metabolism.
